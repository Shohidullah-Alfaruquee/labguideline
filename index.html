Gap Analysis

Our initial assessment identifies the differences between your current processes and the requirements of the desired standard. This comprehensive evaluation helps us understand your organization's starting point and what needs to be implemented.

During this phase, our experts conduct thorough interviews, review existing documentation, and observe current practices to identify areas that require improvement.

Key activities include:

  • Evaluation of existing management systems
  • Identification of compliance gaps
  • Risk assessment and analysis
  • Resource allocation planning
  • Development of preliminary roadmap

Documentation & Implementation

We develop comprehensive documentation tailored to your organization's specific needs and industry requirements. Our documentation includes policies, procedures, work instructions, and forms that align with the standard's requirements.

Our implementation process includes:

  • Developing a customized documentation framework
  • Creating process maps and workflow diagrams
  • Establishing record-keeping systems
  • Implementing control mechanisms
  • Setting up monitoring and measurement processes

We ensure all documentation is clear, concise, and easily understood by your team members.

Training & Awareness

Effective training ensures that your team understands the new processes and their roles within the system. We develop customized training programs based on the specific needs of different organizational levels.

Our training approach combines theoretical knowledge with practical exercises to ensure concepts are understood and can be applied in daily operations.

Training modules include:

  • Executive overview sessions for leadership
  • Implementation training for process owners
  • Role-specific training for employees
  • Internal auditor training
  • Continuous improvement workshops

On-site Consultancy & Training

Our experts work directly with your team at your facility to ensure proper implementation and address any challenges in real-time. This hands-on approach helps to embed the new processes into your organization's culture.

On-site activities include:

  • Process implementation support
  • Internal audit assistance
  • Management review preparation
  • Corrective action guidance
  • Pre-certification assessment

We focus on knowledge transfer to ensure your team becomes self-sufficient in maintaining the system after our engagement.

External Certification Audit

We guide you through the final step of obtaining official certification. Our team prepares you for the audit and supports you throughout the process to ensure a successful outcome.

Our preparation includes a mock audit that simulates the certification process, helping to identify any last-minute improvements and building confidence in your team.

The certification process includes:

  • Selection of accredited certification body
  • Stage 1 documentation review preparation
  • Stage 2 on-site audit support
  • Addressing non-conformities
  • Post-certification maintenance planning

Our comprehensive 5-step service process ensures a smooth transition to certified compliance with minimal disruption to your operations.

ISO Certification Process

Frequently Asked Questions about obtaining and maintaining ISO certification

What is ISO certification and why is it important?
ISO certification is a seal of approval from a third-party body that a company runs to one of the international standards developed and published by the International Organization for Standardization (ISO). It demonstrates that your organization follows industry best practices, improves efficiency, enhances customer satisfaction, and helps meet regulatory requirements.
What are the most common ISO standards?
The most common ISO standards include:
  • ISO 9001: Quality Management Systems
  • ISO 14001: Environmental Management Systems
  • ISO 45001: Occupational Health and Safety
  • ISO 27001: Information Security Management
  • ISO 50001: Energy Management Systems
What are the key steps in the ISO certification process?
The certification process typically involves:
  1. Gap analysis to assess current practices
  2. Development and implementation of required processes
  3. Employee training and awareness
  4. Internal audits to verify compliance
  5. Management review
  6. Selection of a certification body
  7. Stage 1 (documentation review) and Stage 2 (on-site) audits
  8. Addressing any non-conformities
  9. Certification decision and issuance
How long does the certification process typically take?
The timeline varies depending on the organization's size, complexity, and current level of compliance. For small to medium-sized businesses, the process typically takes 6-12 months. Larger organizations may require 12-18 months. Preparation time before the actual certification audit is the most significant factor.
What is the cost of obtaining ISO certification?
Costs vary significantly based on:
  • Organization size and number of locations
  • Scope of certification
  • Current state of compliance
  • Choice of certification body
  • Need for external consultants
For a small business, costs typically range from $5,000 to $15,000. Medium-sized businesses might spend $15,000-$30,000, while large enterprises can exceed $50,000.
How often do we need to renew ISO certification?
ISO certificates are typically valid for three years. During this period, you'll need to undergo surveillance audits (usually annually) to maintain certification. After three years, you'll need to complete a recertification audit to renew your certificate for another three-year period.
What happens during a surveillance audit?
Surveillance audits are conducted annually to ensure continued compliance. They typically:
  • Review changes to the organization and its processes
  • Assess the effectiveness of the management system
  • Verify corrective actions from previous audits
  • Examine selected areas of the standard
  • Check internal audit and management review processes
These audits are shorter than the initial certification audit and focus on maintaining compliance.
Can we implement multiple ISO standards simultaneously?
Yes, implementing multiple standards simultaneously is not only possible but often recommended. Many ISO standards follow the High-Level Structure (HLS), making integration easier. An Integrated Management System (IMS) can combine quality, environmental, safety, and other standards into a unified framework, reducing duplication and improving efficiency.
What are the most common reasons for failing an ISO audit?
The most common reasons for audit failures include:
  • Lack of documented procedures and records
  • Failure to implement corrective actions effectively
  • Inadequate internal audit programs
  • Poor management review processes
  • Non-conformities not properly addressed
  • Employee unawareness of policies and procedures
  • Insufficient evidence of process effectiveness
How do we choose a certification body?
Consider these factors when selecting a certification body:
  • Accreditation status (ensure they're accredited by a recognized body)
  • Industry experience and expertise
  • Reputation and client references
  • Auditor qualifications and consistency
  • Cost structure and value for money
  • Geographic coverage if you have multiple locations
  • Their approach to the audit process and client support
